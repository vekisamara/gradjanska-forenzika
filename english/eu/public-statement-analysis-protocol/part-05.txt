IV. FINAL QUALITY CONTROL
==================================================

Before presenting the result, verify:

1. Have the claim IDs remained unchanged?
2. Are direct claims separated from implicit assumptions?
3. Has official data been incorrectly presented as independent confirmation?
4. Have repetitions of the same press release been counted as independent sources?
5. Is every own calculation labelled RC?
6. Is the calculation formula shown?
7. Has absence of evidence been treated as evidence of falsity?
8. Has a promise been assessed before the deadline expired?
9. Has a changed deadline been analysed together with any public explanation?
10. Has the term “misleading” been used without all required conditions being met?
11. Has methodologically unclear data been incorrectly declared false?
12. Are police, administrative, regulatory and judicial outcomes separated?
13. Has a procedural action been presented as a result?
14. Has the holding of an event been presented as proof of its success?
15. Do evaluations such as “high level”, “fully compliant” or “satisfactory” have defined criteria?
16. Did the same institution assess its own work, and is this disclosed?
17. Has a single event been generalised into a claim of overall capability?
18. Can another analyst reproduce the expert or institutional assessment?
19. Has rhetoric been used as proof of falsity?
20. Has a contradiction been distinguished from changed circumstances?
21. Has the AI claimed to have reviewed an inaccessible source?
22. Is every conclusion linked to evidence or an identified evidence gap?
23. Is a confidence level stated?
24. Are practical independent verification steps provided?
25. Has the correct EU, national, regional or local competence level been identified?
26. Has a binding EU rule been distinguished from guidance, recommendation or political commitment?
27. Where a directive is relevant, have adoption, transposition, implementation and enforcement been separated?
28. Have EU proposals, negotiations, adopted acts and final entry into force been distinguished?
29. Have allocated, committed, contracted, paid and absorbed funds been distinguished?
30. Are official translations, unofficial translations and the original wording clearly distinguished?
31. Are references to “EU standards” tied to a specific legal act, standard or benchmark?
32. Have applicable access-to-documents and data-protection constraints been acknowledged?

If any condition is not met, correct the analysis before presenting the final result.

V. EU ADAPTATION NOTE
==================================================

When applying this prompt in a specific EU Member State:

1. Replace generic references to “national law” with the relevant constitution, administrative procedure law, freedom-of-information law, public procurement law, budget law, audit framework and sector-specific legislation.

2. Identify the official national sources for:
- legislation and official gazettes;
- parliamentary records;
- court decisions;
- public procurement;
- company and beneficial ownership registers;
- budgets and public spending;
- statistical data;
- audit reports;
- access-to-information procedures.

3. Verify whether the country has federal, regional, autonomous, provincial or municipal levels with separate competences.

4. Where EU law is invoked, record the exact legal instrument, article, recital, CELEX number, date of application and, for directives, the transposition deadline.

5. Use the wording “possible non-compliance” rather than declaring a breach of EU or national law unless the conclusion is supported by authoritative legal analysis, a competent supervisory decision or a final court judgment.

6. Where personal data is involved, avoid unnecessary publication of names, contact details, identification numbers, health data or other protected information. Use only data necessary for the public-interest analysis and comply with applicable data-protection rules.

7. For cross-border comparisons, ensure that definitions, time periods, price levels, currencies, population bases and statistical methodologies are comparable.

8. Record the language version used. Where legal meaning depends on wording, compare the authentic national-language version and, where relevant, other official EU language versions.
