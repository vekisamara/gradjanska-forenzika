WHAT THIS PROMPT DOES

This prompt helps citizens examine a public statement, news article or official announcement using evidence, current legal sources and clearly defined verification steps. It can be used for national, regional, local and EU-level matters. It does not replace professional legal advice.

WHAT YOU NEED

Prepare:

1. a link to the article, statement or official publication;
2. the country, place and institution concerned;
3. any document, photograph, earlier statement or other relevant information you have;
4. the date of the analysis.

HOW TO USE IT

1. Copy the complete Quick Citizen Analysis prompt into an AI tool.
2. Complete the INPUT section. Use “unknown” or “none” where information is unavailable.
3. Add this instruction:

“Apply the complete Civic Forensics prompt to the information above. Open and review the supplied link, use current official sources and clearly state all limitations.”

4. Review the result and check whether the cited sources actually support the conclusions.

HOW TO READ THE ASSESSMENTS

CONFIRMED — appropriate evidence supports the claim.

PARTIALLY CONFIRMED — evidence supports only part of the claim.

CONFIRMED ONLY AS OFFICIAL DATA — an institution published the information, but it has not been independently confirmed or reproduced.

UNSUPPORTED — sufficient supporting evidence was not found in the reviewed sources.

NOT VERIFIABLE — the necessary documents, data or methodology are unavailable.

PROMISE — DEADLINE HAS NOT EXPIRED — it is too early to determine whether the promise has been fulfilled.

IMPORTANT EU DISTINCTIONS

Always distinguish between:

- an EU proposal and legislation already in force;
- an EU directive and its national transposition;
- formal transposition and actual implementation or enforcement;
- approved funding, contracted funding and money actually paid;
- expenditure and a demonstrated public result;
- EU, national, regional and local responsibilities.

Useful official sources include EUR-Lex, Curia, Eurostat, TED, national official journals, public procurement portals and EU funding transparency databases.

BEFORE SHARING THE RESULT

Check that:

- the original link was actually reviewed;
- quotations are accurate;
- the headline is not treated as the speaker’s own words;
- official data is not presented as independent confirmation;
- missing evidence is not treated as proof that a claim is false;
- no final legal conclusion is made without reviewing the current applicable law and relevant documents;
- personal and sensitive data has been removed where necessary.
