# 🌐 Future Roadmap: Civic Intelligence Dashboard

This document summarizes the strategic direction of the **Civic Intelligence Dashboard**, a planned AI-assisted platform for monitoring public narratives, institutional decisions and governance risks through structured Civic Forensics methodology.

The detailed research version is maintained in [`research-concepts/VISION.md`](research-concepts/VISION.md).

## Mission

To transform bureaucratic opacity into verifiable civic intelligence by combining public documents, legal reasoning, metadata analysis, structured prompts and dashboard-ready indicators.

## Platform objectives

- **Visual accountability:** convert complex administrative text into clear indicators of institutional conduct.
- **Inconsistency auditing:** compare public statements with legal and administrative outputs.
- **Procedural risk detection:** identify silence of administration, missing reasoning, formalism, ignored evidence and responsibility shifting.
- **Democratic resilience:** track recurring patterns of transparency, opacity, misinformation and institutional avoidance.

## Core modules

1. **Document Intake:** collection of acts, decisions, FOI responses, minutes, public statements and metadata.
2. **Civic Forensics Prompt Layer:** structured prompts for quick triage, deep analysis, FOI drafting, appeals and narrative auditing.
3. **Evidence Graph:** connection between institutions, acts, dates, deadlines, signatories, legal bases and public-interest claims.
4. **Risk Scoring:** structured assessment of formalism, opacity, procedural defects and public-interest risk.
5. **Dashboard Output:** visual indicators suitable for citizens, journalists, researchers, civil society and donors.

## MVP direction

The first MVP should not attempt to solve every governance problem. It should focus on a narrow pipeline:

1. upload or paste an administrative act,
2. anonymize sensitive data,
3. run quick triage,
4. run deep analysis,
5. generate FOI or appeal draft,
6. export structured findings as JSON,
7. visualize the case as a dashboard card.

## Collaboration

The project seeks technical collaborators, civil society partners, legal researchers, AI/data engineers, UI/UX contributors and funders interested in building open-source democratic accountability infrastructure for regional and EU use cases.
